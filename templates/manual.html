1. Overview

Senyasalin is an offline Filipino Sign Language (FSL) learning and recognition system. It uses MediaPipe landmarks and an LSTM model to evaluate signs through your webcam in real time.

Key Features

  • Account-based practice with login-required training pages
  • Learn Mode with category-based sign videos
  • Select Mode for focused practice on a chosen sign
  • Activity Mode for random sign challenges
  • Auto Mode for continuous recognition
  • Distance guidance with live meter and color status
  • Result logging with streaks, daily summaries, and PDF export

Supported Sign Categories

  • Numbers (5 signs)
  • Colors (8 signs)
  • Family (6 signs)
  • Relationship (2 signs)
  • Survival (5 signs)

2. Getting Started

Creating an Account

  1. Click SIGN UP in the navigation bar.
  2. Enter a username and password, then confirm your password.
  3. Click Create Account. You'll be redirected to the login page.

Logging In

  1. Click LOGIN in the navigation bar.
  2. Enter your username and password.
  3. Click Log In. After logging in, LEARN, SELECT, ACTIVITY, AUTO, and RESULTS appear in the navigation bar.

Access Rules

  • Public pages: HOME, ABOUT, USER MANUAL, LOGIN, SIGN UP
  • Login required: LEARN, SELECT, ACTIVITY, AUTO, RESULTS
Tip: The first time you visit each page, an interactive tutorial will appear automatically. You can check "Don't show again" once you're comfortable, or click the ? help button in the navbar to replay it anytime.

System Requirements

  • A device with a webcam (laptop, desktop with external camera, or tablet)
  • Allow camera permissions when prompted
  • Enough CPU/GPU resources for live video processing

3. Learn Mode

Learn Mode is the study page. You choose a category and sign, then watch its demonstration clip.

How to Use

  1. Navigate to LEARN from the navbar.
  2. Select a category card (Numbers, Colors, Family, Relationship, Survival).
  3. Click a sign badge to load that sign video.
  4. Read the short learning steps shown below the video panel.
  5. Repeat with multiple signs before moving to SELECT or ACTIVITY.
Tip: Focus on hand shape, hand height, and movement path. The practice modes use landmark motion, so consistency matters.

4. Select & Practice Mode

Select Mode lets you choose one target sign and practice it repeatedly with immediate feedback.

How to Use

  1. Navigate to SELECT from the navbar.
  2. Pick a category and a specific sign you want to practice.
  3. Click Start Practice and wait through the 1-second prep phase.
  4. Perform the sign during capture (around 5 seconds in standard mode).
  5. Review the result: correct/incorrect plus guidance messages.
  6. Use Show again and Try again when shown after an incorrect attempt.

Mode Options

  • Real-Time Recognition: shorter rolling prediction while signing
  • Show Timer: shows/hides the capture timer bar
  • Show Results: shows/hides the live text feedback
  • Show Visuals: shows/hides landmark visualization overlays

Feedback Behavior

Incorrect attempts can trigger a skeleton/video feedback guide. The app may also show warnings such as No hands detected, Too few frames, or One person at a time please.

Note: Select and Activity save Correct and Incorrect attempts to your results history.

5. Activity Mode

Activity Mode is a challenge flow. The system selects a random sign from supported categories, then you perform it under the same capture logic used in Select Mode.

How to Use

  1. Navigate to ACTIVITY from the navbar.
  2. Click Start Activity.
  3. Read the challenge sign shown in Your Challenge.
  4. Perform the sign during capture and wait for evaluation.
  5. Use Try again to retry the same sign after feedback.
Tip: There is no visible difficulty selector in this build. Difficulty comes from the randomness of selected signs.

6. Auto Recognition Mode

Auto Mode continuously evaluates your signing and appends recognized results to the on-screen history.

How to Use

  1. Navigate to AUTO from the navbar.
  2. Allow camera access if prompted.
  3. Click Start Learning! to begin capture.
  4. Sign naturally while keeping hands visible.
  5. Watch the detection card, result overlay, and history panel for updates.
  6. Click Stop to end the session.

Recognition Thresholds Used by Auto Mode

  • Confidence < 0.70: Unrecognized Sign
  • 0.70 to < 0.92: Incorrect (closest sign hint shown)
  • >= 0.92: Correct
Tip: Enable Real-Time Recognition for faster rolling commits, or disable it for slower, full-window evaluations.

7. Results & Progress Tracking

The Results page summarizes your attempts and trends from the database for the logged-in user.

Summary Dashboard

  • Total Attempts, Correct, Incorrect, and Streak (days)
  • Today summary badge with today correct/incorrect totals
  • Most common sign based on recent data

History Views

You can review latest attempts and a daily activity table (last 14 days) with attempts, correct, and incorrect counts.

Exporting Your Data

Export Results (PDF) supports two scopes: Today only or Full history.

8. Camera & Environment Tips

Distance Guide (Exact Values Used in App)

  • Perfect (green): 0.36 m to 0.38 m (36-38 cm)
  • Acceptable (yellow): 0.33-0.35 m or 0.39-0.41 m
  • Poor (red): below 0.33 m or above 0.41 m
Important: The distance meter is an estimate based on shoulder width and camera field-of-view. Use it as guidance, not a medical-grade measurement.

General Camera Setup

  • Lighting: Face a light source (e.g., a window or lamp). Avoid having bright light behind you.
  • Background: A plain, uncluttered background helps the AI focus on your body.
  • Framing: Keep face, shoulders, and both hands clearly visible.
  • One person only: If multiple people appear, prediction may be blocked.
  • Clothing: Avoid sleeves/backgrounds that blend with your hand color.
  • Stability: Keep the camera steady. A tripod or stable surface is ideal.
Important: When the app asks for camera permission, click Allow. If you accidentally deny it, go to the app's settings to re-enable camera access.

9. Troubleshooting

Camera not working

  • Check that the app has camera permission.
  • Make sure no other application is using the camera at the same time.
  • Try refreshing the page or restarting the app.

Distance warning is always red

  • Move until the meter is near 0.36-0.38 m.
  • Center your shoulders in frame.
  • Ensure only one person is visible.

Low recognition accuracy

  • Improve lighting conditions (see Camera Tips above).
  • Make sure your full upper body and hands are in the frame.
  • Hold the sign long enough to avoid Too few frames.
  • In Select/Activity, use Show again and retry the same sign.

Status says "No hands detected"

  • Raise both hands into the visible frame area.
  • Increase contrast between your hands and the background.
  • Do not move too fast at the start of capture.

Page loads slowly

  • The first load may take a moment while the AI model initializes.
  • Close unused applications to free up system resources.
  • If using a GPU-equipped machine, ensure GPU drivers are up to date for faster inference.

Cannot log in

  • Double-check your username and password for typos.
  • If you've forgotten your password, create a new account.

10. FAQ

Does the app need internet?

No. Senyasalin runs fully offline. All AI models and resources are bundled locally.

What is the recommended camera distance?

The implemented target range is 0.36-0.38 m (green). Yellow ranges are 0.33-0.35 m and 0.39-0.41 m.

What signs are supported?

The system recognizes signs across Numbers, Colors, Family, Relationship, and Survival.

How is my data stored?

Practice results are stored in a local SQLite database used by the app runtime. No external cloud upload is performed by default.
